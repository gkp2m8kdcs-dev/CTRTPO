Frequently asked questions

How does CTRTPO find duplicates?

CTRTPO computes a perceptual signature for each photo on your device and compares those signatures locally. Exact duplicates share identical signatures; similar shots share closely-matching ones. No image ever leaves your iPhone.

Where does my data go?

Nowhere. CTRTPO does not upload photos, metadata, or any analytics to a server. All analysis happens on-device inside Apple's standard Photos framework.

Why does CTRTPO need photo library access?

iOS requires explicit permission to read your library in order to scan it for duplicates, similar shots, and large items. You can revoke this at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy → Photos.

I deleted a photo by accident. Can I get it back?

Yes. Deletions go to the system "Recently Deleted" album for 30 days. Open the Photos app → Albums → Recently Deleted to restore.

Why does storage analysis show different numbers than Settings?

iOS Settings shows total device storage, which includes system files, app data, and cached items. CTRTPO focuses on your photo library specifically, so the two numbers will differ by design.

Does CTRTPO support iCloud Photos?

Yes. CTRTPO reads through the standard Photos framework, which transparently works with iCloud Photos. Items not yet downloaded will appear in results and will download on demand when you open them.

How do I delete the app's local data?

Deleting CTRTPO from your iPhone removes all of its local data, including cached scan results. There is no separate account or cloud backup to clear.

How do I report a bug or request a feature?

Use the contact options below. Please include your iOS version, iPhone model, and a short description (or screenshot) of what happened.
